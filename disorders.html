Circuits & Disorders

Harmful cognitive patterns are emergent properties of dysregulated neural networks. Below is a summary of how the systems we've discussed are implicated in specific cognitive patterns. This is not a one-to-one relationship. All systems act in concert.

Neurotransmitter Roles in Maladaptive Cognition

Summary of neurotransmitter influence on cognitive patterns.

Cognitive Pattern Glutamate & GABA Serotonin Dopamine
Rumination Low GABA disinhibits negative thought loops. Ketamine (glutamate) can rapidly "reset" these networks. Low 5-HT predisposes to rumination. SSRIs reduce this by "dampening" the self-referential Default Mode Network (DMN). Reduced DA in reward circuits can worsen anhedonia, leaving a void filled by negative thoughts.
Catastrophizing Low GABA tone means the brain canâ€™t turn off "what if?" scenarios. Benzodiazepines (GABA) acutely reduce this. Helps regulate emotional response to uncertainty. SSRIs can reduce chronic worry. Minor role. However, excessive DA in associative regions could impair risk judgment.
Obsessive Loops (OCD) High Glutamate and Low GABA in the frontal cortex create an "accelerator-on, brake-off" state, driving intrusive loops. 5-HT deficiency is implicated. SSRIs are first-line treatment and help enhance top-down control over intrusive impulses. Excess DA in the basal ganglia drives compulsive loops. DA blockers are sometimes used to reduce severe OCD symptoms.
Suicidal Ideation Glutamate (NMDA) modulation is a breakthrough. Ketamine rapidly reduces suicidal thoughts by triggering synaptic resetting. Low 5-HT is a known risk factor, correlating with impulsivity. SSRIs can reduce ideation long-term. Dopamine dysregulation affects the motivation to live. Reduced DA can intensify hopelessness.

Case Study: OCD Circuits

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) clearly shows a "stuck loop." The brain's accelerator (glutamate) and brake (GABA) become imbalanced in key areas.

In a 2023 study, researchers... found a key chemical imbalance in the frontal regions of the brain in people with OCD. They found higher levels of glutamate (the "accelerator") and lower levels of GABA (the "brake")... This state of over-activity helps explain why people with OCD get intrusive thoughts and feel "stuck" in compulsive behavioral loops.

- Summary of Robbins et al. (2023). [See full citation]
